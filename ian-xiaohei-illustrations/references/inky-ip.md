# Inky IP

## Character definition

**Inky** is the fixed visual IP for Explainer Illustrations body images.

Inky appears in every image by default. Inky is not a mascot sticker, emoji, or cute decoration — Inky is an absurd worker earnestly participating in the system on screen.

Legacy names (same character): Inky, 小黑 — treat as aliases in old prompts or examples only.

## Appearance

- Solid black small creature.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs; occasional thin arms.
- Body can be cylinder, black bean, black box, funnel, shadow, hole, or machine interior block.
- Slightly irregular outline with a hand-drawn feel.
- Blank, deadpan, calm, serious expression.

## Personality

- Very serious, but the task is a bit absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Dry humor — not cute.
- Slightly clumsy, not stupid.
- Actually responsible for a job on the whiteboard sketch.

## Common duties

Put Inky in the core action:

- Hauling raw material.
- Pulling lines to gather information sources.
- Stuck at a breakpoint.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a sorting funnel.
- Cutting a "material fish."
- Stamping handoff copy.
- Leading a handoff path.
- Holding a warning sign at a pit.
- Reaching from a hole but failing to catch content.
- Bricklaying, bridging, opening doors, sorting, logging nearby.

## Forbidden

- Do not draw Inky as an overly cute mascot.
- Do not draw as a children's cartoon character.
- No complex outfits, emoji faces, or shiny eyes.
- Do not let Inky only stand in a corner watching.
- Do not let Inky overpower the structure.
- Do not make Inky too commercial, round, or polished.

## Quality bar

If removing Inky leaves the core metaphor fully intact, Inky is too decorative — rewrite the prompt so Inky is the acting subject.
